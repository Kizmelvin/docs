Mono is used by businesses that need to meet **data protection and financial regulations**. This page summarises how Mono's practices align with common frameworks so you can complete vendor or compliance questionnaires.

## NDPR (Nigeria)

- **Lawful basis and consent:** User consent is obtained before linking accounts or sharing data. Mono Connect and related flows are designed so that the user explicitly authorises access. See [User Consent](/trust/user-consent) for how consent is captured and how you can explain it to your users.
- **Data minimisation and purpose:** Only the data necessary for the product (e.g. balance, transactions, identity) is requested and processed. Mono does not retain data beyond what is required to provide the service and meet legal obligations.
- **Security and confidentiality:** Data is encrypted in transit and at rest. See [Data Encryption](/trust/data-encryption). Access is restricted and audited.
- **Rights and retention:** Mono supports user and business needs to revoke access and manage data in line with NDPR and your own retention policies.

## GDPR alignment (where applicable)

Where Mono processes personal data of individuals in the EU/EEA, practices are aligned with GDPR principles: lawful basis (including consent), purpose limitation, data minimisation, security, and support for data subject rights where applicable. For specifics (e.g. DPA, sub-processors), contact Mono or your account manager.

## Your responsibilities

- Obtain and document user consent before initiating Connect, Prove, or payment flows.
- Use Mono only for the purposes you have disclosed to users.
- Keep your own records for audits and respond to data subject requests in line with your jurisdiction.

For **encryption and security**, see [Data Encryption](/trust/data-encryption). For **user consent and data privacy**, see [User Consent](/trust/user-consent) and [Data Privacy](/trust/data-privacy).
