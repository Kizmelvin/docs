Mono treats financial and identity data as highly sensitive. This page explains how we encrypt
data at rest and in transit, and how this maps to common regulatory and audit requirements.

## Encryption in transit

- **TLS everywhere**: All traffic to Mono’s APIs and dashboard is encrypted using
  industry‑standard TLS (HTTPS).
- **Modern cipher suites**: We regularly review and phase out weak ciphers in line with
  industry best practices.
- **Certificate management**: Certificates are managed centrally and rotated proactively.

> For your own compliance documentation, you can state that all communication with Mono is
> encrypted in transit over TLS.

## Encryption at rest

- **AES‑256**: Sensitive data (including tokens and financial metadata) is encrypted at rest
  using AES‑256 or equivalent strong encryption.
- **Key management**:
  - Encryption keys are stored in hardened key‑management systems.
  - Access to keys is restricted and audited.
- **Backups**: Encrypted data remains encrypted in backups and log archives.

## Tokenization & access controls

- **Scoped access**: API keys and access tokens are scoped to specific environments and
  applications.
- **Principle of least privilege**: Internal systems and services only receive the minimum
  data required to perform their function.
- **Audit logging**: Access to sensitive data is logged, monitored, and reviewed.

## How this maps to your compliance checklist

When filling in vendor security or compliance questionnaires, you can typically answer:
- Data "in transit" is encrypted using TLS.
- Data "at rest" is encrypted using AES‑256 or equivalent modern cryptography.
- Encryption keys are centrally managed with strict access controls and audit logging.
