Mono’s **Lookup** and **Prove** products use sensitive identity data (e.g. BVN, NIN, government ID). This page summarises how we handle that data so you can answer governance and audit questions.

## Principles

- **Explicit consent:** Users are informed and must consent before their identity is verified or shared. Consent is captured in the Connect, Prove, or payment flow before data is sent to Mono.
- **Purpose limitation:** Identity data is used only to perform the verification or product you requested (e.g. account linking, KYC, mandate creation). It is not used for marketing or unrelated purposes.
- **Minimal retention:** Data is retained only as long as necessary to provide the service, meet legal obligations, and support dispute resolution or audit.
- **Security:** Data is encrypted in transit and at rest. Access is restricted and logged. See [Data Encryption](/trust/data-encryption).

## BVN and NIN

- **BVN (Bank Verification Number)** and **NIN (National Identification Number)** are used only for verification and compliance (e.g. account ownership, KYC). They are not exposed unnecessarily in APIs or logs.
- **Storage and handling** follow applicable regulations (e.g. NDPR) and industry practice. For detailed commitments, use [Regulatory Compliance](/trust/regulatory-compliance) and [Data Privacy](/trust/data-privacy).

## Your obligations

- Only request and use identity data for the purpose you have disclosed to the user.
- Do not store or log full BVN/NIN in plain text in your systems; use the identifiers returned by Mono (e.g. customer ID, account ID) for linking and reconciliation.
- Honour user requests to revoke access or delete data in line with your and Mono’s policies.

For **user-facing consent** and **data privacy**, see [User Consent](/trust/user-consent) and [Data Privacy](/trust/data-privacy).
